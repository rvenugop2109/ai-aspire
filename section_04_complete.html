Year Theme
The foundation year. No intimidation. Maximum curiosity.
Grade 6 opens with no devices for the first 8 weeks. Students build the logical reasoning foundation on paper before a screen appears. By the time AI tools are introduced in October, students have the mental models to use them meaningfully — not just playfully.
Exit Competencies — Grade 6
A student leaving Grade 6 can —
Solve visual, spatial, analogy, and if-then reasoning problems and explain their working
Explain what AI is and how it learns — without jargon — using the pattern recognition framing
Identify at least 5 AI applications in everyday Indian life and describe what each is doing
Write a clear, specific prompt — iterate it based on output quality — and document what changed and why
State one way AI can be wrong and one way it can be unfair — with a real example of each
Name three things they will never share with an AI tool and explain the reasoning

Tool Introduction Approach
In Grade 6, tools are introduced slowly and carefully — observation before participation. Students watch the teacher use the tool for a full session before touching it themselves. The first student hands-on session is always in pairs, with the teacher circulating. No student works on a tool alone in Grade 6.
Compliance Checkpoint
School-managed ChatGPT Edu and Claude for Education accounts must be active and tested before Week 18. Parental consent forms must be signed and on file. Teacher must complete a dry-run session independently before Week 18.
Data Hygiene Rule — Established in Grade 6
From the first tool session: "No real names, no personal details, no school information in any prompt — ever." This rule is taught as a skill, not a restriction, and carries through all four grades.

Year Theme
Inquiry-led. Students form opinions, not just receive information.
Grade 7 shifts from discovery to interrogation. LR is no longer a standalone unit — it is the method applied inside every AI session. Prompting gets a named framework (RCT). Students produce their first real research artefact — the AI Report — presented live to the class.
Exit Competencies — Grade 7
A student leaving Grade 7 can —
Explain how a machine learning model learns — using a non-technical analogy — and distinguish supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning
Read a data chart critically and identify at least one way it could be misleading or misinterpreted
Write a structured RCT prompt for any subject area and evaluate the quality of the output against the prompt's intent
Use Perplexity for research and clearly distinguish between a cited AI result and an uncited generative AI response
Explain why AI hallucinates, identify a deepfake using a structured verification process, and name at least 3 logical fallacies in AI claims

Year Theme
The year students stop being analysts and become builders.
Grade 8 is the most significant shift in the programme. Vibe coding enters — students build real, functional applications by describing what they want in plain English. The Mini-Project in November is the centrepiece: teams of 3 identify a real problem, build an AI-assisted solution on Lovable, and present to a panel.
Exit Competencies — Grade 8
A student leaving Grade 8 can —
Design a documented prompt chain of 4+ prompts for a complex real-world output — with a flowchart showing the logic before execution
Build a functioning interactive web application through vibe coding on Lovable — from systems map to working prototype
Diagnose why a vibe-coded application is not behaving as intended and direct the AI builder to fix it with a precise problem statement
Map the accountability chain for an AI failure and identify where responsibility sits at each node
Write an AI solution brief with fairness safeguards designed in — not retrofitted as an afterthought

Grade 9
EVALUATE & CREATE
"How do I think critically about AI and build with it?"
28

~42 hrs

Jun – Dec

Age 14–15

Year Theme
Students are treated as junior practitioners. The Capstone is the programme's final word.
Grade 9 is the payoff for four years of compounded capability. Sessions are Socratic and self-directed. The individual Capstone Project — a multi-modal, vibe-coded, panel-presented product — is the evidence that earns the AI Foundation Certificate. Every tool, every skill, every year converges here.
Exit Competencies — Grade 9 (Programme Completion)
A student leaving Grade 9 can —
Design and deploy system prompts, few-shot prompts, and meta-prompts — and evaluate prompt quality with practitioner rigour
Build and present a multi-modal AI project: vibe-coded app, AI voice, AI video, AI research base — from problem definition to live panel
Conduct a full ethical review of any AI product brief — harms, failure modes, accountability gaps, and designed safeguards
Steel-man the strongest opposing view on any AI and society question before stating and defending their own position
Articulate the societal implications of AI automation, regulation, and environmental cost — with specific Indian context

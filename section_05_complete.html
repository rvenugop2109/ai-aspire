The Landscape Approach — How Every Tool Category Is Introduced
20·40
The Pedagogy

See all options first.
Choose based on merit.

Every tool category is introduced through a 20-minute teacher-led landscape demo before any student creates an account. Students observe three to four tools from the same category, doing the same task, side by side. Then they choose their primary tool — and log their reasoning.

🕐
20 min landscape · 40 min primary
The session structure for every tool introduction across all four grades
🧠
Students build judgment, not habits
Seeing the landscape first prevents tool lock-in — students understand why they chose, not just what they use
📋
Every choice is documented
Tool Choice Log maintained from Grade 7 — submitted as a Capstone artefact in Grade 9
How It Works — Every Tool Category Introduction
1
Landscape Demo — Teacher-Led
Teacher opens 3–4 tools from the same category with identical briefs. Students observe output quality, interface, and trade-offs. No student accounts yet.
2
Compare & Discuss — Class Dialogue
Which tool produced the best output for this brief? Which was fastest? Which gave the most control? Students form and defend their own view before a primary tool is named.
3
Primary Tool Named — Hands-On Begins
The programme recommends a primary tool for each category. Students then spend 40 minutes using it — first in pairs, then independently as confidence builds.
4
Tool Choice Log Updated
Students document the category, the tools demoed, the primary tool chosen, and their own reasoned justification. This entry becomes part of their Grade 9 Capstone Design Package.
The Tool Choice Log
A three-year running document — started in Grade 7, updated at every landscape demo, submitted in Grade 9. It is the evidence that a student's tool decisions were deliberate, reasoned, and self-directed — not just whatever the teacher last demonstrated.
